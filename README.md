# Protocol Field

The Protocol Field is a diagnostic framework for mapping the dynamic state of protocols in practice. It treats any structured interaction as a protocol, from 
formal technical standards to informal social norms, and plots them on two experiential axes: Adaptability (the protocol's capacity to evolve) and Opacity 
(how transparent or strange it is perceived to be by its users).

Rather than classifying protocols into fixed categories, the framework maps how they are enacted, how they drift under the gravity of protocol debt, and how they 
shape human capacity in increasingly AI-mediated systems. It is designed to diagnose where coordination is becoming brittle, opaque, or both, before the 
consequences become unrecoverable.
